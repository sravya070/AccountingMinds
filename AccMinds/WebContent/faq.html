Frequently Asked Questions

What is ICWAI?

The Institute of Cost Accountants of India (Previously Known as The Institute of Cost and Works Accountants of India) was established by a special act of Parliament, namely, the Cost and Works Accountants Act, 1959 as a statutory professional body for the regulation of the profession of cost and management accountancy.

What is the Course structure ?

The course is structured in 3 levels of competency

  • Foundation Course: LEVEL-A

    • Eligibility for Admission: 17 years of age and Passed Senior Secondary Examination under 10+2 scheme of a recognized Board( Incumbents waiting for the result can apply for provisional admission)

  • Intermediate Course: LEVEL-B

    • Eligibility for Admission: 18 years of age and passed degree Examination of any recognized University or equivalent( Incumbents waiting for the result can apply for provisional admission)

  • Final Course: LEVEL-C

    • Eligibility for Admission: Passed Intermediate Exam of ICWAI

When to apply ?

Exams are held two times in a year i.e. on 11th to 18th June and on 10th to 17th December. For June Term Examination apply before 30th November of previous year and for December Term Examination apply before 31st May.

What is the duration of the Course? Or How many years its take to become a CWA or CMA? Is it difficult?

There is no fixed duration for the course. Since the course is structured in groups, students can appear for exams group wise and complete the course. However, the number of times a student can appear for a group will be limited.
